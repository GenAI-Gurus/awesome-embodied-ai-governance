# Contributing

Thank you for helping improve Awesome Embodied AI Governance.

## What belongs here

A resource should help practitioners govern AI systems that perceive, decide,
or act in the physical world. Strong submissions address at least one of:

- Embodied AI governance research or surveys
- Machine or robot safety
- AI management, assurance, or impact assessment
- Public regulation, official guidance, or incident-reporting regimes
- Runtime permissions and policy enforcement
- Identity, authentication, or attestation
- Secure software updates or supply-chain integrity
- Human oversight, override, or emergency intervention
- Telemetry, provenance, audit, or incident evidence
- Simulation, fault injection, or safety evaluation
- Security threats specific to autonomous or embodied systems
- Maintained reference implementations

## Quality bar

- Prefer official sources, primary research, and maintained open source.
- Link to the canonical project or publication page.
- Explain the governance relevance in one concise sentence.
- Verify that the link works.
- Place the item in the narrowest applicable section.
- Prefer resources that can support repeatable engineering, assurance, policy,
  or incident-learning work.
- Avoid duplicate entries, affiliate links, unauthorized copies of paid
  standards, broad vendor marketing, and promotional superlatives.

## Description style

Use concise, factual descriptions. Prefer:

```markdown
- [Resource name](https://example.com) - What it contributes to embodied-AI
  governance.
```

Avoid vague descriptions such as "great resource", "comprehensive platform", or
"must-read". If a resource makes a strong safety or compliance claim, summarize
what it provides rather than endorsing the claim.

## Pull request format

In the pull request, briefly explain:

1. Why the resource belongs in this list.
2. Whether you are affiliated with it.
3. Which section you placed it in and why.
4. When you last verified the link.

## Research submissions

For papers, link to the publisher, proceedings, DOI, or arXiv abstract. Include
associated code, data, or benchmark links only when they are maintained and
useful.

Prefer papers that are directly about embodied AI governance, safety, security,
assurance, evaluation, oversight, policy, or incident learning. General robotics
capability papers belong only when they materially change the governance
problem, such as transferable foundation models, fleet learning, or autonomous
tool use.

## Regulation and standards

Link to official government, regulator, standards-body, or standards-catalogue
pages. If a standard is under development, say so clearly.

Do not upload or link to unauthorized copies of paid standards. Link to the
standards body's official catalogue page.
